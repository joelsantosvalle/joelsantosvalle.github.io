I am currently completing my Master's degree in Computer Science at the University of Würzburg, where my research focuses on multimodal learning. I am fortunate to be supervised by Humboldt Prof. Dr. Radu Timofte and Dr. Zongwei Wu. During my Master's studies, I have also had the opportunity to work as a working student at Bosch, where I worked at the Engineering Localization Technology Department, and at Fraunhofer IGCV, where I worked for the FORSocialRobots project.

I received my Bachelor's degree in Mechatronics from the Technical University of Applied Sciences Würzburg-Schweinfurt, where I primarily focused on robotics under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Tobias Kaupp. During this time, I also completed a research internship at service robotics lab, where I was supervised by Prof. Dr. Marco Schmidt and worked on object handover from a robot to a human.

Research

My research broadly focuses on multimodal learning and world models, with the long-term goal of enabling robots to operate reliably in cluttered and perceptually challenging environments. I have worked with several robotic platforms, most recently on audio-visual scene understanding for robots.
